How these numbers are calculated

Walk-forward backtest (how performance is measured)

The model is evaluated using expanding-window walk-forward validation: at each monthly cutoff, we train on every sample available before that month and test on samples from that month only. No look-ahead. No random shuffling. Across 46 monthly folds, the model is trained and re-evaluated from scratch — what you see is what the model would have produced in real time.

Each fold trains a 30-model bootstrap ensemble (30 LightGBM quantile regressors on 30 different bootstrap resamples). The median prediction across the 30 models is the point estimate; the 25th–75th percentile of the 30 predictions is the confidence band.

Net return (the outcome each prediction is scored against)

Every prediction is compared to a friction-realistic outcome — what an investor would actually keep after fees:

net_return = (sell_price × (1 − 0.13 FVF) − $5 shipping) / buy_price − 1
with 5% buy slippage + 3% sell slippage applied

eBay final-value fee, shipping label cost, and realistic buy/sell slippage are all baked in. A prediction is profitable (a "hit") only if net_return > 0 — the investor actually makes money after all costs.

Conviction hit rate (top-1% / top-2%)

Of all predictions across all 46 walk-forward folds, we take the top 1% or top 2% by predicted return — the model's highest-conviction picks. Hit rate is the % of those picks that achieved net_return > 0. This is the most investor-relevant metric: "when the model tells you to buy, how often are you actually right?"

Top-2% Sharpe ratio

Sharpe for the top-2% cohort = (monthly_mean_net_return / monthly_std) × √12. Higher Sharpe = stronger risk-adjusted returns. Rules of thumb: > 1.0 is good, > 2.0 is strong, > 3.0 is rare in public markets. Computed only on months with at least 3 picks (for statistical stability).

Spearman rank correlation

Correlation between the model's ranking of cards and the actual ranking of realized returns. Unlike R² it doesn't require predicting exact returns — just putting winners above losers. Pokemon card returns are noisy; a Spearman of 0.5 means the model ranks cards meaningfully better than random.

R-Squared (out of sample)

The standard R² computed on held-out walk-forward predictions. Lower than Spearman by design: this domain is noisy and the model's value is in ranking, not in exact return prediction. Positive R² on future returns in collectible markets is respectable; > 0.10 is strong.

Market base rate (context, not model skill)

Of every card in the investable universe (top ~1,000 by median PSA 10 price ≥ $100), what % end up net-positive over 180 days after all friction costs. This is a property of the market, not the model. A typical investor picking randomly inside this universe would experience this hit rate. The model's job is to beat this baseline meaningfully at the conviction tip, which it does by 3–4×.

Confidence band & HIGH / MED / LOW labels

Each card's confidence band is the 25th–75th percentile spread across the 30 bootstrap models. Tight band = models agree; wide band = they disagree. Bucketing calibrated meaning-first: LOW is reserved for the top ~8% of cards by disagreement, so the label actually means "needs caution," not "mild noise."

  • HIGH — band width < 5 percentage points (tight model agreement, ~62% of cards)
  • MED — band width 5–8 points (normal disagreement, ~30%)
  • LOW — band width ≥ 8 points (conspicuously uncertain, ~8%)

Investable universe (which cards get scored)

The model scores only cards with median PSA 10 price ≥ $100 across their price history (~1,000 cards). Below that threshold, eBay's 13% fee + $5 shipping eat 18% before any return shows up — friction would dominate signal. Cards below the threshold simply don't get projections; their detail pages still show price, grading, and pack odds. Training and deployment use the exact same universe so the walk-forward metrics above accurately reflect what an investor will experience.

Feature set (25 features across 6 categories)

Price action: 30/90/365-day returns, peak discount, trough recovery, volatility, moving-average distance, log price. Demand pressure: 7/30-day net flow, demand pressure, supply saturation, demand/supply ratio. Grading supply: PSA 10 population, PSA 10 gem rate. Cultural ceiling: iconic-Pokemon score, franchise tier, per-Pokemon all-time peak (log + ratio). Rarity & history: rarity tier, history depth. Reprint risk: count of recent same-Pokemon releases, shock-decay adjustment.

What the model can't see

Every card detail page also surfaces a "Where this model fails" block. The model has no input for: sentiment (Reddit/Twitter volume, Google Trends), cultural catalysts (anime airings, tournament results, artist collabs), reprint announcements before they ship, sudden PSA population shocks, macro/whale liquidity moves. When any of those drive a card, the model will miss it. Use the projection as a quantitative second opinion, not a replacement for qualitative judgment.

Promotion gate (why a model reaches this page)

A newly-trained model does not automatically reach investors. It must first pass five named thresholds on its walk-forward metrics: top-2% hit rate > 70%, top-2% net return ≥ 10%, top-2% Sharpe > 1.5, Spearman > 0.10, and at least 30 top-2% picks in the test set. Models that fail are shelved; models that pass are promoted and serve projections to the UI. The v2.0 model passed all five by a wide margin.
